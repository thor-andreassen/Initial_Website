Research Interests

Digital Twins for Orthopaedics

Development of computational models that simulate musculoskeletal behavior to aid in diagnosis and treatment planning.

Finite Element Modeling

Creating detailed computational models of bones, joints, and soft tissues for biomechanical analysis.

In Vivo Data Capture

Using high-speed stereo radiography to capture real-world joint kinematics and validate computational models.

Computational Biomechanics

Modeling and simulation of musculoskeletal systems to understand joint mechanics and pathology.

Subject-Specific Modeling

Developing personalized computational models based on individual patient anatomy and physiology.

Open Science and Reproducibility

Providing research tools, data, and software to the scientific community to advance research.
